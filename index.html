About Us

Our project, 3StalSat, is dedicated to studying the differences in crystal growth between Earth and microgravity conditions. By launching a CanSat to simulate near-space environments, we aim to uncover how low gravity impacts the structure and development of crystals.

Our Progress

Our progress at 3StalSat has focused on designing and developing a CanSat to investigate the effects of microgravity on crystal growth. We are now planning our mission to launch the CanSat into near-space, where it will simulate low-gravity conditions for the experiment.

Applications For Our Missions Experiment

We believe that crystals formed in microgravity have a better crystalline structure, which can significantly enhance their applications in medicine and electronics. In microgravity, the absence of gravitational forces allows for more uniform crystal growth, resulting in fewer defects and impurities. This leads to clearer and more precise crystals that can improve the efficacy of pharmaceutical compounds, ensuring better drug delivery and stability. In electronics, high-quality crystals are essential for the development of more efficient semiconductors and photonic devices, enabling faster and more reliable performance. The refined crystalline structure enhances conductivity and minimizes energy loss, paving the way for advancements in technology and medical therapies. As we explore these potential applications, the unique environment of space presents exciting opportunities for innovation in both fields.
